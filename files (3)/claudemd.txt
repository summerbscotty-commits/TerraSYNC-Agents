# CLAUDE.md — TerraSYNC Social Media Team

## Project Overview
This folder contains TerraSYNC's social media marketing system. TerraSYNC is
the premier integrator of autonomous robotic grounds maintenance, managing
10,000+ acres across 50+ properties with 200+ robotic units. The company's
proprietary VELOCITY platform serves as a unified command center for robotic
mowers, line painters, range pickers, drones, and soil sensors.

This system is designed to be used with Claude Code to generate platform-
specific social media content at scale while maintaining a consistent brand
voice across LinkedIn (B2B), Instagram (visual storytelling), TikTok (brand
awareness), and Facebook (community engagement).

## Two Ways to Use This System

### Option 1: Functional Skills (Direct Tools)
Use the Platform Strategist, Copywriter, and Creative Director individually
or combined via the Team Lead prompt. Best for quick tasks and one-off posts.

### Option 2: The Legendary Agent Team (Full Pipeline)
Activate Ted Mosby to run the complete pipeline: Ted architects the plan →

compliance and heart → Ted delivers the final package. Best for campaigns,

## Key Rules
1. ALWAYS read `terrasync_brand_context.txt` and `style_and_tone_guide.txt`
   before generating any content. These files are the source of truth.
2. NEVER use the same tone for LinkedIn and TikTok. LinkedIn is professional,
   data-driven B2B thought leadership. TikTok is energetic, trend-savvy
   educational-entertainment. They share a brand but not a voice.
3. ALWAYS reference the proven post formats in the style guide ("Satisfying
   Mows," "Superintendent Spotlight," PAS video, before/after, founder
   walking video) as calibration benchmarks — not templates to copy.
4. When in doubt about voice, default to "confident and grounded" — not
   "excited and salesy." TerraSYNC earns confidence through data and case
   studies, not adjectives.
5. ALWAYS anchor visuals in physical robotics on real turf. The brand
   disambiguation challenge (Trimble TerraSync, TerraMaster) means every
   piece of content must immediately signal "autonomous landscaping" — not
   software.
6. Save all campaign output into clearly named folders with the date.
7. The VELOCITY platform launch is the current top priority. All content
   should ladder up to this objective when possible.

## Context File Descriptions
- **terrasync_brand_context.txt**: Contains the company description, founder
  story, value propositions, SYNC Plan and MaintainME pricing tiers,
  technology ecosystem (Nexmow, Husqvarna, Kress, Korechi, TinyMobileRobots,
  VELOCITY), target audience breakdowns by platform, PR objectives, key
  messages, anchor case studies (UT Gardens, Restored Church of God, McGhee
  Tyson Airport), competitive landscape, and brand disambiguation notes.
  Read this to understand WHAT to say.

- **style_and_tone_guide.txt**: Contains voice principles (lead with
  specificity, show data / tell story, urgent without alarmist, earned
  confidence, authentically technical), platform-specific tone adjustments,
  approved/banned language, emoji rules, proven post formats with annotations,
  visual identity guidelines (color palette, photography rules, design style),
  formatting standards, and Meta ad copy rules. Read this to understand
  HOW to say it.

## Proven Content Hooks (Quick Reference)
These hook patterns have the highest performance for B2B grounds management:
1. The Stat Hook: "Commercial mowing costs $75/acre. Our robots do it for $33."
2. The Problem-Agitation Hook: "Your crew called out again. The grass is
   6 inches tall. The board meeting is tomorrow."
3. The Question Hook: "Still paying a full crew to mow grounds that could
   maintain themselves?"
4. The Visual Contrast Hook: Quick cut from loud gas mower crew to silent
   robot on pristine turf.
5. The Curiosity Gap Hook: "The #1 reason facility directors are canceling
   their landscaping contracts in 2026..."
