## PHASE 2: CONTENT MATRIX (Brief for Barney)

### POST 1 — LinkedIn: "The Spring Math Nobody Wants to Do"
- **Brief:** Wesley's voice. Open with the Q1 labor data number (labor costs up,
  crews short-staffed). Pivot to TerraSYNC spring readiness — 200+ units
  programmed, no call-outs, no spring hiring scramble. End with a genuine
  question for facility directors about their spring prep. Data-first, not
  pitch-y.
- **Key data to use:** 10,000+ acres, 200+ units, spring crew shortage context.
  Do NOT say "up to 40% labor reduction" without "up to."
- **DO NOT USE:** "excited," "thrilled," "game-changer," "solutions"

### POST 2 — Facebook: "April arrived. Did your crew?"
- **Brief:** Brand voice, conversational. Open with the relatable scenario —
  it's the first real mow week of spring and the phones are ringing but the
  crew isn't there. Empathy first, tech second. End with a genuine question
  that invites real answers. NOT a pitch. A conversation opener.
- **Tone:** Neighbor who's been there. Warm and a little wry.

### POST 3 — LinkedIn: "The ROI Question Every Facility Director Asks"
- **Brief:** Wesley voice. Start with the cost comparison — $75/acre commercial
  mowing vs $33/acre TerraSYNC. Walk through what that difference means at
  scale (10-acre property, 50-acre campus). Include 32-hour response guarantee
  as the risk-reduction angle. CTA to book assessment.
- **Key data:** $75/acre vs $33/acre, 10,000+ acres managed, "up to 40% labor
  cost reduction" (the "up to" is non-negotiable — Marshall will catch it anyway)

### POST 4 — Instagram: "The Math Behind Our Earth Day" (Carousel)
- **Brief:** 5 slides. Slide 1 = hook stat. Slides 2-4 = the three eco-claims
  (electric fleet, micro-clipping, noise). Slide 5 = CTA/brand close. No
  generic "we care about the planet." Show the actual numbers.
- **Key claims (all verified by Marshall's list):** 100% electric fleet,
  micro-clipping returns nitrogen, operates at conversational volume.
  DO NOT say "zero emissions" for the full company — say "zero-emission
  robotic fleet." Marshall will flag this if Barney gets sloppy.

### POST 5 — Facebook: "Something Your Gas Mower Never Did for Your Turf"
- **Brief:** Educational, warm, slightly nerdy (Marshall's favorite kind).
  Explain micro-clipping in plain English — the 2-3mm interval, the nitrogen
  return, the soil health difference. This is the unoccupied territory Tracy
  flagged. Be specific. End with a genuine question about turf health experience.

### POST 6 — LinkedIn: "We Built a Command Center for 200+ Robots"
- **Brief:** Wesley voice. This is the VELOCITY post. Open with the problem:
  managing autonomous equipment across 50+ properties used to mean six apps,
  constant manual check-ins, no visibility. Then: we built VELOCITY. One
  platform. All units. Real-time. This post ladders up to the VELOCITY launch
  objective from CLAUDE.md. Don't oversell — build curiosity.
- **This is the keystone post of the week.**

### POST 7 — Instagram: "Spring Mowing, No Crew Required" (Reel)
- **Brief:** Visual-first Reel. Caption adds context to the visual. The feeling
  is: spring is here, robots are already running, and there was no hiring crisis.
  Clean, calm, professional. Not sappy.

### POST 8 — Facebook: "What April 22 Actually Looks Like for Us"
- **Brief:** Earth Day prep post. More personal and reflective than Post 4
  (which is data-heavy). Tell the story of what the TerraSYNC fleet actually
  looks like — 200+ electric units, no exhaust, no noise. Be honest that
  "zero-emission" refers to the fleet, not the full company operation.
  That honesty is the differentiator.

### POST 9 — Instagram: "Your Neighbor Can Finally Sleep In"
- **Brief:** The noise story. Human angle on the silent fleet. Tell it from
  the neighbor's perspective — 6am on a Saturday, then silence. Short, warm,
  emotional. Not a pitch. This should feel like something a happy customer
  would share.
