Here's something a gas mower never did for your turf:

Fed it.

When our robots mow, they cut at 2–3mm intervals — much finer than a
traditional rotary blade pass. Those tiny clippings don't sit on top
of the lawn and turn yellow. They decompose back into the soil almost
immediately, returning nitrogen naturally.

It's called micro-clipping, and it's why the turf at properties we
manage tends to get healthier over time, not just consistently maintained.

Less synthetic fertilizer. Better soil structure. Turf that actually
responds to the season instead of just surviving it.

The science has been around for years — the robotic mowing frequency
is what makes it practical at scale.

For those of you who manage turf professionally or just care about the
health of your grounds: has your soil health changed since you moved
to more frequent mowing? We'd genuinely love to hear your experience.

Leave a comment — the turf nerds on our team will definitely respond. 😄
